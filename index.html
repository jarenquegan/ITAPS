Prioritization Matrix

Objectives

  • To assess the status of ICT equipment in the different provincial offices and divisions and provide recommendations based on the findings and observations.
  • To objectively assess all ICT equipment as the basis for equitable distribution/redistribution of ICT equipment (laptops, etc.).

Criteria

The following criteria components have been established to ensure that every division and provincial office creates the prioritization report objectively. This report gives lower scores to components that need to be prioritized or replaced. After the scoring of each criterion, the scores are added together, and the equipment with the lowest overall score is given the greatest priority.

Score Interpretation

Total Score Range: 5 (lowest) to 18 (highest)

Priority Ranking: Equipment is ranked from lowest to highest total score. Lower scores indicate higher priority for replacement or upgrade.

Example: A laptop with Intel i3 (1), HDD (1), 4GB RAM (1), 6 years old (1), and Physical Damage (2) receives a total score of 6 points and would be prioritized for replacement.
